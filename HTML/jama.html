OVERVIEW
Jama Masjid, located in the heart of Old Delhi, is one of the largest and most iconic mosques in India. Built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan between 1650 and 1656, this grand architectural marvel is renowned for its majestic red sandstone and marble structure. With its impressive courtyard, towering minarets, and intricately designed domes, the mosque stands as a testament to Mughal architectural brilliance. It can accommodate thousands of worshippers and is a significant center for religious and cultural gatherings. The Jama Masjid not only serves as a vital place of worship but also attracts visitors from around the world who come to admire its historical grandeur and serene ambiance.
